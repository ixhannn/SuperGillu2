## Design Context

### Users
Lior is used by couples sharing a private relationship space on mobile. In Our Room, they are building a shared digital corner they can decorate together, revisit often, and slowly fill with relationship milestones, notes, and gifts.

### Brand Personality
Playful, affectionate, game-like. The interface should feel warm and collectible, with a sense of progress and shared ownership instead of glossy luxury polish.

### Aesthetic Direction
For Our Room, use a retro social-sim / handheld game direction inspired by pixel-art life sims: compact HUD bars, bright toy-like controls, and a framed room stage that feels like the main play surface. Prefer light mode with warm peach and lavender tones, and avoid generic glass-card romance UI.

### Design Principles
1. The room is the hero; secondary information must stay compact.
2. Decorating and collecting should feel faster than reading menus.
3. Notes and gifts should support the room, not compete with it.
4. Progress, presence, and unlocks should read like game signals, not dashboard analytics.
5. Every visual element should feel intentionally playful and tactile.
